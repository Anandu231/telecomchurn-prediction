Understanding Customer Churn

  • What is Customer Churn? The rate at which customers stop doing business with a company
  • Why is it Critical? Customer acquisition costs 5-25x more than retention
  • Industry Impact: Telecom companies lose 15-25% of customers annually
  • Our Goal: Build a predictive model to identify 'at-risk' customers

Addressing the Class Imbalance Challenge

Technical Challenge:

Dataset exhibited severe class imbalance (90% non-churn vs 10% churn)

Impact:

Standard models biased towards majority class, leading to low recall for churners

Why it Matters:

Missing true churners means lost retention opportunities

Strategic Solution: SMOTE & TabNet

SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Over-sampling):

What: Generate synthetic samples for minority class

Why: Balance dataset, improve model's ability to learn churn patterns

TabNet Architecture:

What: Deep learning for tabular data with attention mechanism

Why: Superior performance, handles mixed data types, interpretable
